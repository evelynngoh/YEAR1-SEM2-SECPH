Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) course provided valuable understanding of user experience (UX) design principles, interaction design processes, and usability evaluation techniques in interactive system development. Throughout the course, knowledge of human behaviour, cognitive aspects, user-centred design, prototyping, usability testing, and evaluation methods was strengthened through lectures, assignments, and project-based learning activities. The hands-on prototype development project enhanced creativity, analytical thinking, and problem-solving skills while providing practical exposure to UX design processes such as requirements gathering, conceptual design, sketching, prototyping, and user evaluation. In addition, teamwork, communication, and collaboration skills were improved through group discussions, peer assessments, presentations, and iterative project development activities. The course also increased awareness of the importance of designing accessible, user-friendly, and effective systems that meet user needs and improve overall user satisfaction. Overall, this course successfully enhanced both theoretical understanding and practical competency in human-computer interaction and UX design.
